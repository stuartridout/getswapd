The insight

Your camera roll has three tiers.

Most of what you take never goes anywhere. Not because you don't want to share it — because there's nowhere right.

1%
The top

The curated photos. Good lighting, flattering angle, ready for Instagram. A tiny, performed sliver of your actual life.

30%
The middle

The real ones. Ugly-laughing, cry-laughing, mid-blink, messy, honest. Too candid for Instagram, too personal for the group chat. Nowhere to go. Until now.

60%
The bottom

Screenshots, receipts, parking spots, your son's homework, shopping lists. Logistics and clutter, occasionally useful.

How it works

Both answer. Then both reveal.

You and one other person answer the same question. Separately. Neither of you sees the other's answer until you've both gone. Then both are shown at once. Mutual exposure is the whole trick — you can send the ugly photo because you know they're about to send theirs too.

It gets better the longer you do it.
